# 🧠 Jonny Valdez -- Coaching Framework

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1️⃣ Core Philosophy

My coaching system is built around **mechanisms, not exercises**.

Instead of asking: \> "What workout should we do?"

We ask: \> "What stimulus does this client need right now?"

Training decisions are based on: - Strength curves\
- Resistance profiles\
- Stability demands\
- Loadability\
- Fatigue cost\
- Desired adaptation

Precision \> Randomness.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2️⃣ The 3 Pillars of Hypertrophy

### 1. Mechanical Tension (Primary Driver)

-   Heavy loading
-   Mid-range emphasis
-   Higher systemic fatigue

### 2. Metabolic Stress

-   Shortened range bias
-   Higher volume
-   Lower systemic fatigue

### 3. Muscle Damage

-   Lengthened bias
-   Controlled eccentrics
-   Moderate loading

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3️⃣ Strength Curves

Muscles are strongest in **mid-range**\
Weaker in: - Fully lengthened - Fully shortened

All three ranges are trained strategically.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4️⃣ Resistance Profiles

### Free Weights

-   Gravity dependent
-   Heaviest at peak vertical displacement
-   Inconsistent tension

### Cables

-   More consistent tension
-   Adjustable line of pull

### Machines

-   Cam-based design
-   Most stable
-   Ideal for maximizing output safely

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5️⃣ Exercise Selection Filters

Before selecting an exercise:

1.  Does the resistance profile match the strength curve?
2.  Is fatigue cost appropriate?
3.  Does it accelerate progress?
4.  Is complexity appropriate for the client?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6️⃣ Stability → Output Model

### Skill-Based

-   Movement literacy
-   Higher frequency
-   Lower load

### Output-Based

-   Mechanical tension focus
-   Heavier loading

### Isolation Finishers

-   Shortened bias
-   Volume accumulation
-   Low systemic fatigue

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7️⃣ Planes of Motion

1.  Sagittal -- Flexion/Extension\
2.  Frontal -- Side-to-side\
3.  Transverse -- Rotation

Complexity progresses with competency.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8️⃣ Fatigue Management Principles

-   Volume = Time under load\
-   More reps often \> more sets\
-   Increasing load ≠ guaranteed hypertrophy\
-   Quality of effort drives adaptation

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9️⃣ Kettlebell Integration

-   Improves stability and athleticism\
-   Offset loading increases core demand\
-   Builds grip strength\
-   Enhances positional control

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🔥 Coaching Standard

My job is not to: - Make workouts hard\
- Make clients tired

My job is to: - Deliver precise stimulus\
- Manage fatigue\
- Build resilient humans\
- Drive sustainable hypertrophy
